# Results and Discussion control checklist

## Purpose

This checklist ensures that the final report is organized around biological and computational questions, not around scripts.

The course guide asks that the Results section describes the results obtained, explains what they mean, and uses tables/figures where appropriate. The Discussion should explain issues encountered, differences from the original method, and advantages/disadvantages.

## Results must answer these questions

1. Does O67940_AQUAE remain unresolved in current curated annotation?
2. What family/domain evidence exists today?
3. Does AlphaFoldDB provide a suitable model for structural comparison?
4. Does structural comparison favor 2Q6O or 1RQP?
5. Does global sequence identity resolve specificity?
6. What do the key functional residues suggest?
7. What evidence supports cautious annotation transfer?
8. What limitations appear directly from the results?

## Results should include

- sequence and metadata provenance;
- domain annotation;
- structural model/source comparison;
- structural alignment/comparison;
- sequence alignment;
- residue mapping;
- annotation-transfer interpretation;
- concise reproducibility audit evidence where relevant.

## Discussion must emphasize

1. What remains biologically supported.
2. What remains uncertain.
3. What was gained by modern re-analysis.
4. What reproducibility problems were encountered.
5. Why transparent computational workflows matter in bioinformatics.
6. How this project differs from the original workflow.
7. Why the final interpretation remains computational and cautious.

## Writing rule

Do not organize the report as a list of scripts.

Organize it as an evidence chain leading to a cautious biological interpretation.
